Contrast with modular pipelines (perception $\to$ prediction $\to$ planning). Discuss advantages (joint optimization, simplicity) and challenges (interpretability, data hunger, generalization).}

\textit{Behavioral cloning setup: given expert demonstrations $\{(o_t, a_t^*)\}$, learn $\pi_\theta(a|o)$. Discuss open-loop vs.\ closed-loop evaluation. Explain why open-loop PDMS is a useful proxy metric but has known limitations.}

\subsection{The NAVSIM Benchmark}
\label{sec:bg-navsim}

\textit{Describe NAVSIM: built on nuPlan logs, provides multi-camera images + LiDAR + ego state. Splits: navtrain, navtest. Sensors available. Evaluation via PDM Score (PDMS) and Extended PDM Score (EPDMS). Explain PDMS sub-metrics:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item No-at-fault Collision (NC)
    \item Drivable Area Compliance (DAC)
    \item Ego Progress (EP)
    \item Time-to-Collision (TTC)
    \item Comfort
\end{itemize}

\textit{Note that NAVSIM does not natively provide per-city splits --- this is a gap our work addresses.}

\subsection{Joint-Embedding Predictive Architectures (I-JEPA)}
\label{sec:bg-ijepa}

\textit{Describe I-JEPA: predicts representations of masked image patches from visible context, without pixel reconstruction. Context encoder + predictor architecture. Key property: learns semantic features without relying on hand-crafted augmentations. We use ViT-H/14 (630M params) and ViT-S/14 variants.}

\subsection{DINOv2}
\label{sec:bg-dinov2}

\textit{Self-distillation with no labels. Student--teacher framework with centering and sharpening. Trained on LVD-142M curated dataset. ViT-L/14 variant. Discriminative SSL.}

\subsection{Masked Autoencoders (MAE)}
\label{sec:bg-mae}

\textit{Generative SSL: mask 75\% of patches, reconstruct pixels via asymmetric encoder--decoder. ViT-L/16 variant. Discuss the hypothesis that pixel-level reconstruction may not yield the most transferable planning features.}

\section{Planning Architectures}
\label{sec:bg-planning}

\subsection{TransFuser}
\label{sec:bg-transfuser}

\textit{Multi-modal fusion architecture: ResNet-34 camera backbone + ResNet-34 LiDAR backbone + GPT-style auto-regressive fusion. Outputs waypoints via MLP regression head. Baseline in our cross-city benchmark.}

\subsection{Latent World Model (LAW)}
\label{sec:bg-law}

\textit{LAW (ICLR 2025): adds a latent world model as an auxiliary training loss to TransFuser. LID depth encoding for 3D-aware features. Single-trajectory waypoint prediction. Achieves ~84.6 PDMS on NAVSIM. Our primary test bed for SSL backbone swapping.}

\subsection{Truncated Diffusion Planning (DiffusionDrive)}
\label{sec:bg-diffusiondrive}

\textit{DiffusionDrive (CVPR 2025): instead of denoising from Gaussian noise, starts from K-means anchor trajectories and applies only 2 denoising steps. 45+ FPS inference. 88.1 PDMS. Base architecture for our JEPA-Diffusion planner. Describe the anchor generation, noise schedule, and multi-scale cross-attention decoder.}

\subsection{DiffusionDriveV2 and RL Post-Training}
\label{sec:bg-ddv2}

\textit{DiffusionDriveV2 (Dec 2025): adds GRPO (Group Relative Policy Optimization) post-training to DiffusionDrive. Intra- and inter-anchor reward shaping prevents mode collapse. 91.2 PDMS / 85.5 EPDMS. Relevant to RQ6 on RL transfer.}

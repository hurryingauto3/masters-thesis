\section{Summary of Contributions}
\label{sec:concl-summary}

\textit{This thesis investigated the role of visual representations in cross-city generalization for end-to-end autonomous driving. We summarize our contributions:}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{NAVSIM City Splits}: We created the first per-city benchmark for NAVSIM, partitioning scenarios across Boston, Pittsburgh, Las Vegas, and Singapore. This benchmark enables systematic evaluation of cross-city transfer that was previously impossible on NAVSIM. Released as a community resource alongside our IROS 2026 submission.

    \item \textbf{Cross-City SSL Generalization}: Through 19 backbone configurations across TransFuser and LAW architectures, we demonstrated that self-supervised visual representations (I-JEPA, DINOv2, MAE) reduce cross-city generalization gaps by an order of magnitude compared to supervised ResNet-34. This is the central finding of the thesis: \textbf{cross-city failure is a representation problem, not a planning architecture problem}.

    \item \textbf{DiffusionDrive + SSL}: We integrated SSL backbones into DiffusionDrive, achieving up to 86.9 PDMS (MAE, C+L) and demonstrating a camera-only variant at 86.6 PDMS (ResNet-34). Cross-city evaluation of DiffusionDrive + SSL tests whether the representation-centric finding extends to generative planners.

\section{Key Findings}
\label{sec:concl-findings}

\textit{Actionable takeaways for practitioners:}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{SSL pre-training is the single most impactful change for cross-city robustness.} Swapping a supervised ResNet backbone for a frozen SSL ViT backbone, with no other changes, reduces generalization gaps by $\sim$10$\times$.

    \item \textbf{The in-distribution cost is small; the OOD gain is large.} SSL backbones achieve $\sim$1pp lower PDMS in-distribution but maintain performance under cross-city transfer where supervised baselines collapse.
    % TODO: Fill in concrete numbers from Phase 3

    \item \textbf{Frozen backbones generalize better than fine-tuned.} Fine-tuning improves in-distribution performance but may overfit to city-specific features. Frozen SSL backbones act as implicit regularization.

    \item \textbf{Camera-only planning is viable with SSL features.} SSL features partially compensate for removed LiDAR information, enabling edge-deployable planners without expensive sensors.

\section{Limitations}
\label{sec:concl-limitations}

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Open-loop evaluation only.} All results use PDMS/EPDMS, which are open-loop proxy metrics. Closed-loop simulation (e.g., in CARLA or nuPlan simulator) would provide stronger evidence of real-world generalization.

    \item \textbf{Limited geographic diversity.} NAVSIM/nuPlan covers only 4 cities (3 US + Singapore). Broader evaluation across more diverse geographies (Europe, developing countries, varied climate zones) is needed to fully validate the SSL generalization claim.

    \item \textbf{ImageNet pre-training.} Most SSL backbones were pre-trained on ImageNet, not driving data. While our nuScenes-pretrained I-JEPA shows competitive results, a comprehensive study of pre-training data domain is left for future work.

    \item \textbf{No weather/lighting variation.} Our cross-city protocol isolates geographic shift but does not independently test weather or lighting robustness, which is a complementary domain shift axis.

    \item \textbf{Camera-only constraint.} While motivated by edge deployment, removing LiDAR sacrifices 3D geometry information that may be critical in certain scenarios (e.g., occluded pedestrians, narrow alleys).
\end{itemize}

\section{Future Work}
\label{sec:concl-future}

\subsection{Video SSL Backbones (V-JEPA)}
\textit{Extend to video-based SSL (V-JEPA 2) that exploits temporal structure across consecutive frames. Hypothesis: temporal prediction pre-training aligns better with the planning task than single-frame SSL, as driving is inherently a temporal prediction problem. Drive-JEPA's strong results (93.3 PDMS) using V-JEPA motivate this direction.}

\subsection{Closed-Loop Evaluation}
\textit{Evaluate JEPA-Diffusion in closed-loop simulation (CARLA, nuPlan simulator) to validate that open-loop PDMS improvements translate to improved driving behavior. Closed-loop evaluation would also enable direct testing of safety-critical cross-city failure modes.}

\subsection{Driving-Specific Pre-Training at Scale}
\textit{Pre-train I-JEPA on large-scale driving video datasets (nuScenes, Waymo Open, ONCE) instead of ImageNet. The hypothesis: driving-specific pre-training would learn road-relevant invariances (lane markings, vehicle dynamics) that ImageNet pre-training cannot capture, further improving cross-city transfer.}

\subsection{Scaling to More Cities and Datasets}
\textit{Extend city splits to Waymo Open Dataset (San Francisco, Phoenix, etc.) and test cross-dataset transfer (train on nuPlan cities, test on Waymo cities). This would test whether SSL features generalize not just across cities within a dataset, but across different data collection pipelines.}

\subsection{RL Post-Training for Cross-City Transfer}
\textit{Fully investigate whether GRPO/DPO gains transfer across cities (RQ6). If RL overfits to city-specific reward landscapes, develop city-agnostic reward formulations. If gains transfer, SSL + diffusion + RL becomes a complete recipe for robust planning.}

\subsection{Multi-Modal JEPA Encoders}
\textit{Explore JEPA-style pre-training that jointly encodes camera and LiDAR in a shared latent space, potentially capturing complementary information while maintaining the generalization benefits of SSL.}

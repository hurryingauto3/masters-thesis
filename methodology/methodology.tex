\section{Planning Architecture I: LAW with Swappable Backbones}
\label{sec:meth-law}

\textit{Describe the LAW (Latent World Model) architecture as the test bed for the cross-city SSL study (RQ1--RQ3). Key components:}

\subsection{Architecture Overview}
\textit{Per-view spatial decoders, LID depth encoding, delta waypoint prediction, TransformerDecoder world model. The backbone is the only component we swap; everything else remains fixed.}

\subsection{Training Configuration}
\textit{Adam optimizer, lr=1e-4, no weight decay, no scheduler, 20 epochs, batch\_size=32. Backbone lr\_mult=0.1 when fine-tuning. No gradient clipping. Mixed precision (fp16).}

\subsection{TransFuser Baseline}
\textit{We also evaluate on the simpler TransFuser architecture (ResNet-34 camera + ResNet-34 LiDAR + GPT fusion) to test whether cross-city findings are architecture-dependent.}

\section{Planning Architecture II: JEPA-Diffusion}
\label{sec:meth-jepa-diffusion}

\textit{Describe the novel camera-only planner combining I-JEPA with truncated diffusion (based on DiffusionDrive). This addresses RQ4 and RQ5.}

\subsection{Architecture Design}
\label{sec:meth-jd-arch}

\textit{Key modifications to DiffusionDrive:}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Strip LiDAR branch}: Remove all \texttt{lidar\_pc} and \texttt{bev\_raster} dependencies.
    \item \textbf{Replace ResNet-34 with I-JEPA ViT-S/14}: Frozen encoder, outputs $(B, N, C)$ patch tokens.
    \item \textbf{Feature adapter}: Reshape ViT tokens to spatial feature maps $(B, C, H, W)$ with lightweight FPN for DiffusionDrive's \texttt{MultiScaleCrossAttention}.
    \item \textbf{Retain}: K-means anchor trajectories (256 clusters), 2-step truncated diffusion, trajectory decoder.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Anchor Trajectory Generation}
\label{sec:meth-anchors}

\textit{Describe K-means clustering on training trajectories to produce 256 anchor trajectories (\texttt{trajectory\_anchors\_256.npy}). These serve as the denoising starting point instead of Gaussian noise, enabling just 2 diffusion steps at inference.}

\subsection{Training and Inference}
\label{sec:meth-jd-training}

\textit{Training: full navtrain, 20 epochs, batch 128, Adam lr=1e-4. Inference: 2 denoising steps, targeting 45+ FPS. Compare with ResNet-34 + same diffusion head as ablation to isolate SSL contribution.}

\section{Experimental Design}
\label{sec:meth-exp-design}

\subsection{Phase 1: Single-City Training (16 runs)}
\label{sec:meth-phase1}

\textit{Train each of 4 backbones on each of 4 cities = 16 runs. Evaluate each checkpoint on all 4 city test sets = 64 evaluations. Produces a $4 \times 4$ generalization matrix per backbone. Addresses RQ1 and RQ2.}

\subsection{Phase 2: Leave-One-City-Out (16 runs)}
\label{sec:meth-phase2}

\textit{Train each backbone on 3 cities, test on the held-out city. 4 backbones $\times$ 4 held-out cities = 16 runs. Validates Phase 1 findings under a more realistic multi-city training regime.}

\subsection{Phase 3: Data Efficiency (20 runs)}
\label{sec:meth-phase3}

\textit{Train at \{10\%, 25\%, 50\%, 75\%, 100\%\} of data for the best and worst backbone from Phase 1. Tests graceful degradation (RQ3).}

\subsection{Phase 4: JEPA-Diffusion Evaluation}
\label{sec:meth-phase4}

\textit{Train JEPA-Diffusion and ResNet-Diffusion on full navtrain. Report PDMS and EPDMS. Then run the cross-city protocol (single-city training + LOCO) with both to test RQ5.}

\section{Metrics}
\label{sec:meth-metrics}

\textit{For every experiment, we report:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{PDMS} (overall) and sub-scores: NC, DAC, EP, TTC, Comfort
    \item \textbf{EPDMS} (extended evaluation, where applicable)
    \item \textbf{Generalization gap} $\Delta_\text{gen}$
    \item \textbf{Inference speed} (FPS) for JEPA-Diffusion
    \item All results tracked via Weights \& Biases
\end{itemize}

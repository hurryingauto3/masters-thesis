\section{Self-Supervised Learning for Autonomous Driving}
\label{sec:rw-ssl-driving}

\textit{Survey SSL methods that have been applied to driving tasks. Note that most work applies SSL to perception (object detection, segmentation, depth estimation) rather than end-to-end planning. Discuss:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textit{Pre-training on ImageNet vs.\ driving-specific data (nuScenes, Waymo)}
    \item \textit{Contrastive methods (MoCo, SimCLR) applied to driving}
    \item \textit{JEPA-family methods in driving: Drive-JEPA (Jan 2026) uses V-JEPA for planning, achieving 93.3 PDMS / 87.8 EPDMS, but requires LiDAR and does not evaluate cross-city transfer}
    \item \textit{Gap: no systematic comparison of SSL backbones for planning generalization}
\end{itemize}

\section{End-to-End Planning on NAVSIM}
\label{sec:rw-navsim-planners}

\textit{Review the NAVSIM leaderboard landscape and key methods:}

\subsection{Regression-Based Planners}
\textit{TransFuser, LAW, Ego-MLP baselines. Discuss their architectures and PDMS results. Note reliance on supervised ResNet backbones.}

\subsection{Diffusion-Based Planners}
\textit{DiffusionDrive, DiffusionDriveV2, CTG++. Discuss the shift from regression to generative planning and its advantages (multi-modality, better coverage of trajectory space).}

\subsection{Camera-Only Planners}
\textit{PRIX (87.8 PDMS / 84.2 EPDMS) as the best camera-only method with EPDMS. CaRT architecture. Discuss the camera-only constraint for edge deployment and cost reduction. Note: no camera-only method combines SSL + diffusion + EPDMS.}

\subsection{State-of-the-Art Multi-Modal Methods}
\textit{Drive-JEPA, PRIX (full), and other top entries. Contextualize where our work fits in the performance landscape.}

\section{Cross-Domain Generalization in Driving}
\label{sec:rw-cross-domain}

\subsection{Weather and Lighting Robustness}
\textit{Prior work on sim-to-real, day-to-night, clear-to-rain transfer. These are important but orthogonal to geographic shift.}

\subsection{Geographic / Cross-City Transfer}
\textit{Limited prior work. Some studies in object detection (e.g., training on US data, testing on Singapore). To our knowledge, no prior work studies cross-city transfer for end-to-end planning on NAVSIM. Our NAVSIM city splits are the first benchmark contribution in this space.}

\subsection{Domain Generalization Techniques}
\textit{Brief overview of DG methods (data augmentation, domain randomization, meta-learning, invariant risk minimization). Discuss why we take a simpler approach: changing the representation (SSL) rather than the training procedure, and show it is surprisingly effective.}

\section{Reinforcement Learning for Planning Refinement}
\label{sec:rw-rl-planning}

\textit{Discuss RL post-training for driving planners:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textit{DiffusionDriveV2's GRPO: +3.1 PDMS improvement over supervised baseline}
    \item \textit{DPO (Direct Preference Optimization) applied to trajectory ranking}
    \item \textit{Gap: nobody has tested whether RL fine-tuning gains transfer across cities (our RQ6)}
\end{itemize}

\section{Summary and Positioning}
\label{sec:rw-summary}

\textit{Summarize the gaps this thesis fills:}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item No method combines JEPA SSL + truncated diffusion + camera-only + EPDMS evaluation.
    \item No cross-city generalization benchmark exists for NAVSIM.
    \item No study compares SSL backbone impact on planning generalization (only perception tasks).
    \item No study tests whether RL post-training gains transfer across geographic domains.
\end{enumerate}
